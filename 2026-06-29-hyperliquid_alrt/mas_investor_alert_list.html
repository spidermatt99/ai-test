2.0.1. Assessment of Crypto/Blockchain Listings Trend

Based on the historical data from the MAS Investor Alert List, listings of crypto-related companies have seen a significant rise over the last few years, both in absolute counts and as a percentage of total warnings:

  1. Low Historical Presence (Pre-2021): Prior to 2021, crypto-related alerts were relatively rare, making up less than 10% of annual listings in most years (e.g., 8.5% in 2019 and 7.1% in 2020).
  2. Surge in 2021-2022: There was a notable surge in 2021 and 2022, with crypto warnings jumping to 15.8% (16 of 101) and 20.4% (10 of 49) respectively, reflecting the 2021 bull market and subsequent proliferation of crypto projects and scams.
  3. Recent High Platform (2024-2026): After a dip in 2023 (5.7%), warnings have shot up to their highest rates yet:
    • In 2024, crypto-related alerts accounted for 25.6% (10 out of 39) of all listings.
    • In 2025, they reached 27.9% (19 out of 68) of all listings.
    • In 2026 (up to June), they stand at 25.0% (3 out of 12) of listings.
  4. Conclusion: Listings of crypto companies are indeed on the rise in recent years. In the 2024-2026 period, roughly 1 in 4 (25-28%) companies added to the MAS Investor Alert List is a crypto/blockchain-related entity, compared to less than 1 in 10 historically. This indicates that MAS has intensified its scrutiny of unregulated cryptocurrency platforms (such as Hyperliquid, Bybit, KuCoin, and MEXC which were all added recently) and that crypto scams/unregulated offerings continue to represent an increasing share of the alerts.
