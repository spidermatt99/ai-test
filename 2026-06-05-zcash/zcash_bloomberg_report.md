# Privacy Haven Under Siege: Zcash Rout Threatens Crypto Winter’s Best Performers

**By Bloomberg News**  
*June 5, 2026*

(Bloomberg) -- For much of the grueling "crypto winter" of 2026, privacy coins were the industry’s unexpected fortress. While Bitcoin and Ethereum languished under macroeconomic pressure, Zcash and Monero had emerged as defensive havens, significantly outperforming the broader market. That narrative unraveled on Friday.

Zcash (ZEC) plunged more than 20%, tumbling to a daily low of $505 in New York, as the discovery of a "critical" cryptographic flaw in its Orchard shielded pool sparked a wave of panic selling. The retreat marks a sharp reversal for Zcash, which had rallied over 110% earlier this year—a performance that, even after today’s rout, remains more resilient than the double-digit YTD declines seen in many traditional crypto benchmarks.

### The AI-Accelerated Plunge
The velocity of the decline was intensified by the very technology many hoped would stabilize the market. AI-driven high-frequency trading algorithms, programmed to react to "security" and "vulnerability" keywords in developer disclosures, triggered a cascade of automated liquidations. These bots were blamed for a series of flash crashes that saw Zcash briefly touch $410 on several exchanges before human traders could intervene.

"We are seeing a collision of two worlds: cutting-edge zero-knowledge cryptography and AI-powered market mechanics," said Marcus Holloway, head of digital asset strategy at Arca. "The AI bots don't care about the long-term math; they see a risk signal and exit in milliseconds, which is why we saw a 20% move in what felt like a heartbeat."

### Soundness vs. Sentiment
The crisis stems from a "soundness bug" discovered by researcher Taylor Hornby. The flaw theoretically allowed for the unauthorized creation of coins within the Orchard pool. While the Zcash Foundation has stated there is no evidence of exploitation, the inability to verify the supply of a private ledger has historically been the "nuclear option" for investor trust.

In a move to restore confidence, the Foundation announced Friday it would deploy **AI-enhanced forensic auditing tools** to conduct a "transfer audit." These new AI models are designed to scan blockchain metadata for anomalies that could indicate hidden inflation without compromising the cryptographic privacy of individual users.

### A Sector Under Pressure
The contagion has already begun to leak into Monero (XMR), which saw its 2026 "privacy alpha" trimmed by a 9% decline on Friday. Despite the carnage, some analysts point out that the privacy sector’s fundamental outperformance during the 2026 downturn suggests a shift in investor priorities toward censorship-resistant assets.

"Privacy was the only thing working this year," said Elena Petrova, a senior analyst at CoinShares. "Even with a 20% haircut today, Zcash is still weathering the storm better than Bitcoin was at its April lows. But the 'trust-less' math just became a lot more expensive to maintain."

Zcash was trading at $508 as of 4:55 PM in New York, down 21.5% on the day.
