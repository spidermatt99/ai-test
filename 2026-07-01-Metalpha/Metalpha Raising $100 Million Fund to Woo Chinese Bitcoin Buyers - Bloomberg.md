The Supreme Court’s [decision](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-29/supreme-court-lets-fed-s-lisa-cook-stay-in-job-for-now) Monday to leave Federal Reserve Governor Lisa Cook in her job for now is a blow to the Trump administration’s monthslong crusade to fire her over alleged mortgage fraud.

It also marks a personal setback for Acting Director of National Intelligence and [Federal Housing Finance Agency](https://www.bloomberg.com/quote/3218437Z:US) Director Bill Pulte, the pugilistic loyalist who launched the failed effort to oust Cook last summer as part of a wider set of attacks against perceived enemies of President Donald Trump.

Trump stunned Washington in June when [he appointed Pulte](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-02/trump-taps-pulte-as-acting-director-of-national-intelligence), who turned the normally sleepy FHFA into a clearinghouse for flashy mortgage-fraud probes, to the DNI role. The 38-year-old construction heir has no military or intelligence expertise, leaving critics to surmise that it was his experience as a [top administration attack dog](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-08-22/fhfa-s-bill-pulte-gets-trump-s-attention-with-post-on-fed-s-lisa-cook) that won him the job overseeing the nation’s 18 intelligence agencies.

Yet for all of the publicity Pulte has generated in going after perceived opponents, none of the high-profile criminal referrals he made have succeeded in court so far, nor have they cost any prominent officials their jobs.

The FHFA did not have an immediate comment.

The court stopped short of saying whether the allegations, if true, would be sufficient grounds for removing Cook in the midst of her 14-year term. Chief Justice John Roberts said the court was ruling “on narrow grounds.” Cook has denied wrongdoing.

Pulte responded to the ruling by saying, “As I have repeatedly said, I believe Lisa Cook will be indicted for mortgage fraud,” in a [social media post](https://x.com/pulte/status/2071600521996034120 "Click to view webpage.") shortly after the decision was released.

“The ultimate question of whether the President can remove Cook for cause will depend in part on the underlying facts,” Roberts wrote in the 5-4 decision. “In this opinion, we have not addressed the facts, as they have yet to be found or analyzed under the relevant legal standards.”

A federal judge in September issued an order allowing Cook to remain in her position while her challenge to Trump’s effort to fire her proceeded. A divided panel of the US Court of Appeals for the District of Columbia Circuit left that order in place as the challenge continues, with the [majority saying](https://media.cadc.uscourts.gov/orders/docs/2025/09/25-5326LDSN2.FINAL.pdf "Click to view webpage.") “Cook’s due process claim is very likely meritorious.”

### Other Referrals

New York Attorney General Letitia James, a Trump critic, was indicted in October on charges that she secured a cheaper mortgage by misrepresenting a Norfolk, Virginia, property, following a referral by Pulte. A federal judge dismissed the charges the next month, and two separate grand juries declined to re-indict her.

Pulte also launched an investigation into Democratic Senator Adam Schiff of California, who managed Trump’s first impeachment trial as a member of the House, over the financing of a Maryland residence. The status of the case is unclear, although the Justice Department launched a probe into the way it was handled. The [Government Accountability Office](https://www.bloomberg.com/quote/0234468Z:US) has also initiated an investigation into whether Pulte misused public resources to surface the allegations.

The FHFA oversees [Fannie Mae](https://www.bloomberg.com/quote/FNMA:US) and [Freddie Mac](https://www.bloomberg.com/quote/FMCC:US), the government-controlled companies underpinning the mortgage market, and assesses risk on their books. Second homes are considered riskier than primary residences, a factor in their underwriting, and Pulte cast potential occupancy fraud on loans sold to the government-sponsored enterprises in dire terms.

“Do not declare two principal residences in President Trump’s America. Mortgage fraud is a serious crime and must be prosecuted as such,” he wrote in a [post on X](https://x.com/pulte/status/1958158594374873358?s=20 "post on X") last year.

Pulte, whose agency has no authority over the Fed, also pushed a doomed effort to force former Fed Chair Jerome Powell to resign – a move that backfired, with a defiant Powell choosing to stay on as a governor rather than leave when his chairmanship ended last month.

Pulte’s presence and [influence in Washington](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-11-20/fhfa-chief-bill-pulte-becomes-a-potential-liability-for-donald-trump) have been more defined by the pressure campaigns than by his day job. His tactics have occasionally been ham-fisted, such as when he [called a courthouse press conference](https://x.com/pulte/status/1963183430771818746?s=20 "Click to view webpage.") about the Cook matter in September – only to [call it off](https://x.com/pulte/status/1963286261780681088?s=20 "Click to view webpage.") the day before “out of respect for the process.”

### DNI Role

The high court’s decision on Cook comes amid an impasse between the White House and Congress over Pulte’s new role as acting spy chief. A key surveillance authority expired this month as Democrats protested Trump’s decision to name Pulte to the role, and Republicans have also expressed concern. Senate Majority Leader John Thune, a Republican of South Dakota, cautioned that the DNI should not be “weaponized” and pushed to confirm a full-time director before Pulte took over on June 19.

Trump at first appeared to be looking to skirt the blowback over Pulte, quickly [nominating Jay Clayton](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-11/trump-taps-wall-street-top-cop-jay-clayton-as-intelligence-chief) to take over the position full-time. But he bristled at Congress’s efforts to do an end-run around the acting appointment, [wondering on social media](https://truthsocial.com/@realDonaldTrump/posts/116749870028302766 "Click to view webpage.") why Democrats appeared “so afraid of Bill Pulte at DNI??? He would only be Acting!”

Hours before Clayton was scheduled to appear for his confirmation hearing June 17, Trump said he was canceling the hearing. Senate Intelligence Chairman Tom Cotton, Republican of Arkansas, [called off the hearing](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-17/cotton-moves-forward-with-clayton-hearing-as-trump-demands-delay) after he said Trump told Clayton not to appear.
